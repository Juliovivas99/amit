### SLIDE 7: Our Proposed Solution

**Unified Real Estate Operations Platform**

A custom internal operating system — **communications hub first**, then three dashboards:

0. **Communications Hub** *(built first)* — Unified workspace, email visibility, notes, collaboration, search, initial AI-assisted workflows
1. **Acquisition Dashboard** — Deal pipeline, broker tracking, notes, documents, basic analysis
2. **Property Management Dashboard** — Portfolio visibility, occupancy, collections, cash flow, Yardi/Entrata data
3. **Internal Management Dashboard** — Tasks, follow-ups, accountability, executive summaries

**The first objective is to centralize communications and create a unified operational workspace. The dashboard modules are then layered on top of that foundation.**

> **Speaker Notes:** This is not an enterprise replacement project. Communications come first because that's the primary pain point. Dashboards follow once the unified workspace is in place.

### SLIDE 12: Design Principles

2. **Integrate, don't replace** — Yardi, Entrata, CoStar stay; we build the layer on top

5. **Right-sized for five users** — Not an overbuilt enterprise system
6. **You own the code** — Full ownership upon payment

### SLIDE 21: Option 1 — Fixed Price

**$60,000–$80,000 | ~12–16 weeks to production-ready V1 (contractual)**

The final quote within this range depends on confirmed scope, number of integrations, reporting requirements, AI functionality, data migration needs, and implementation complexity identified during discovery.

- Defined deliverables and locked requirements per statement of work

### SLIDE 23: Why We Recommend the Phase 1 Engagement

**We recommend the Phase 1 Engagement — $8,000/month (3-month initial commitment, $24,000).**

While a fixed-price engagement is available, our recommendation is to begin with the Phase 1 Engagement. At this stage, several important variables are still unknown — M365 integration feasibility, communication workflow priorities, and how the team will use the platform day to day.

Rather than investing significant time and budget defining every requirement upfront, the Phase 1 Engagement delivers a working communications workspace quickly, validates the highest-priority workflows, and supports future investment decisions based on actual usage rather than assumptions.

This approach reduces project risk, accelerates time-to-value on the communications layer, and creates a stronger foundation for dashboard modules, integrations, automation, and AI capabilities.

For projects where requirements are fully defined and unlikely to change, fixed price may be more appropriate. Based on discovery to date, the Phase 1 Engagement provides the best balance of flexibility, speed, and long-term value.

> **Speaker Notes:** Be direct but fair to fixed price. "Fixed price is a real option when scope is locked — we're recommending Phase 1 because your communications priority is clear and the MVP path de-risks the investment."

## Presentation Tips

### Do

- Lead with their communications pain point, not your solution
- Reference specific discovery findings about Outlook/Teams/email fragmentation
- Acknowledge risks and integration uncertainty openly
- Present fixed price honestly — it requires locked scope
- Recommend the retainer clearly and explain why communications-first sequencing fits their priority
- Leave 15 minutes minimum for questions

### Don't

- Oversell AI capabilities — be honest about iteration needs
- Position dashboards as the first deliverable — communications comes first
- Position this as a huge enterprise system
- Promise specific CoStar/Yardi integration depth without validating API access
- Present fixed price as equally preferred to retainer
- Use jargon without context
- End without clear next steps
